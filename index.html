Point of View of Child with ASD

To Highlight the Importance of Early Identification and Intervention

Through Spreading Awareness and Knowledge

 

 

Earlier identification leads to earlier diagnosis which in turn leads to early intervention. Early identification is important because it is the initial stepping stone to reducing the negative impact that Autism Spectrum Disorder, ASD, may have on the child as an adult. The developmental period for typical children is already significant to children’s future experiences as an adult, so it may be even more important for children with ASD who need more support to reach similar goals during the same developmental period. Society, especially those involved in early childcare and education, becoming more knowledgeable in ASD will allow the children with ASD to be more likely to receive the support they need.
